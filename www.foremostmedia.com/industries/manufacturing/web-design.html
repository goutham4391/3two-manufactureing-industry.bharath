Website Design for Manufacturing Companies

The best manufacturing website designs and marketing strategies impact the bottom line by driving traffic and increasing lead conversion. Your website is a clear statement of your business' manufacturing capabilities. It can even help you attract employees.

A Manufacturing Website that Wows

While having a beautiful, well-designed website isn’t everything, it sure helps draw the attention of users and can increase leads and/or sales. At Foremost, we pride ourselves on highly-functional, uber-secure websites that also keep viewers intrigued at every scroll and click.

That’s why our customers fall in love with our websites — the combination of functionality and crisp design while also understanding the psychology of a website visitor. So, we welcome you to bring your ideas and dreams for your website. We’re not new to this, so chances are we can not only listen to your idea, but also add some value and perspective.

Manufacturing Website Design Necessities

There are certain design elements that every manufacturing website needs. Without them, your website might leave customers searching for answers they won’t find, might detract them from returning to your site, or might not lead them toward converting. We can help make sure your website has the essential elements, including but not limited to:

Clear and well-thought-out navigation and site map

When a user lands on your website, can they quickly maneuver around to find the information, services, and products they are looking for?

High-resolution photos and videos of products and services

The products or services you offer are unique to your business, so it is important to find high-quality ways to showcase them.

Mobile-friendly design

More and more people surf the web via phone and tablet every day, so your site needs to work well whether someone finds you on desktop or mobile.

Appealing visual design

Everything on your site should align with your most recent branding. The colors, fonts, logos, images, and mnore should be consistent with any other marketing materials.

Clear goals and calls to action

Are you encouraging users to buy? To request a sample? To fill out a form? Whichever route you choose, it should remain consistent throughout the site.

Quality content

Content might not exactly sound like a design issue, but basing a website's images and layout on quality content, rather than basing the content off the design, is most ideal. And having products, downloads, tutorials, locators, and other useful content will keep users coming back.
